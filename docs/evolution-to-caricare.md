# From Vitalyst to CariCare

## Vitalyst: the original EHR prototype

Vitalyst Healthcare Portal explored how clinics could digitise patient administration and clinical records. Its architecture centred on healthcare organisations, staff roles and clinic-scoped access. It included patient registration, medical histories, appointments, results, audit logs, clinic administration and early subscription concepts.

## What the prototype revealed

A clinic EHR can improve internal recordkeeping, but it does not by itself solve fragmentation between hospitals, clinics, laboratories, pharmacies and paper-based systems. A patient may still have multiple disconnected records, and access can fail when they visit another provider or island.

## CariCare: the next product direction

CariCare reframes the solution as a patient-controlled health passport and interoperability layer. The proposed agentic workflow adds:

- Document ingestion and OCR
- Identity binding and duplicate resolution
- Longitudinal timeline construction
- Clinician-facing health summaries
- Human-reviewed AI workflows
- QR-based access with identity fallback
- Cross-provider and eventually cross-island portability

The Vitalyst code is therefore retained as evidence of the team's earlier product development and domain exploration, not as the final CariCare architecture.
